Slide 2: Problem Understanding

SmartHostel

Existing Challenges

Hostel residents frequently face issues related to:

  • Wi-Fi outages & electrical failures
  • Plumbing problems & cleanliness concerns
  • Infrastructure maintenance bottlenecks

Currently, complaints are managed through informal channels such as WhatsApp groups, phone calls, and manual registers.

Problems with Existing System

  • Complaints get lost or forgotten
  • No prioritization mechanism (FIFO queues)
  • Slow response times & lack of transparency
  • No performance analytics or status tracking
Objective

Develop a centralized digital complaint management platform that intelligently routes, prioritizes, tracks, and analyzes hostel complaints.

Slide 3: Proposed Solution

SmartHostel

Smart Hostel Complaint Management System consists of three major modules:

Student Portal

Allows students to register complaints, track status in real-time, view their complaint history, and receive active updates.

Council Dashboard

Allows council members to view incoming complaints in prioritized order, manage lifecycle stages, and monitor hostel workloads.

DSA Sandbox

An educational visualizer showing exactly how Max-Heaps, Graphs, and Hash Maps operate behind the scenes to optimize the platform.

Core Workflow Steps

  1. Student submits complaint details.
  2. Processing Engine performs simulated NLP classification.
  3. Priority score (1-4) is assigned automatically based on keywords.
  4. Complaint enters the Max-Heap Priority Queue.
  5. Dijkstra's routing graph determines department route.
  6. Council processes complaint and chats with student.
  7. Status updates and messages are reflected to users in real-time.

Slide 5: DSA Implementation Details

SmartHostel
Data Structure Implementation & Purpose Complexities & Benefits
1. Priority Queue
(Max Heap)		 Prioritize complaints based on severity.
- Urgent Plumbing -> Priority 4 | Wi-Fi Outage -> Priority 3
- Fan Repair -> Priority 2 | General Feedback -> Priority 1		 Complexities: Insert: O(log n) | Delete: O(log n) | Peek: O(1)
Benefits: Fast scheduling. Urgent issues handled first.
2. Hash Map Provides instant complaint lookup mapping ticket IDs to objects.
- COMP-2301 -> Complaint Details
- COMP-2302 -> Complaint Details		 Complexities: Search: O(1) | Insert: O(1)
Benefits: Fast retrieval, efficient status tracking.
3. Graph + Dijkstra Route complaints to correct department nodes.
- Path traversal: Hostel -> Central Switch -> IT Department		 Complexities: Dijkstra: O(E log V)
Benefits: Shortest path routing, future scalability.
